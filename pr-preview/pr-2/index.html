Language as zip

Language is what happens when you zip a whole mental universe into one thin line of text. Someone else unzips it with a different brain and never gets your exact state, only their reconstruction.

Sentences as memes

Each sentence is basically a meme: a compressed pattern that their mind inflates into a full story, feeling, or frame. Change the brain and the decompression produces a different simulation, even if the words stay the same.

📎 Coinage

The same coinage: evolutionary biologist Richard Dawkins in The Selfish Gene (1976) built meme from Greek mimema (“that which is imitated”) and shortened it to rhyme with gene, stressing memes as cultural analogues of genes.

🏛️ What they become

Religions, ideologies, social hierarchies, and shared fictions like money or nation‑states work this way. Once they take root, they guide behavior, attract followers, punish dissent, and defend against criticism.
