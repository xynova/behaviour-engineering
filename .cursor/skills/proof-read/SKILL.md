# Proof-read and Polish Workflow

## What this skill is for

This skill is a strict editorial filter. It transforms dense, academic, or jargon-heavy text into punchy, visual, and accessible language that hooks a casual reader without talking down to them.

## The 4-Step Editorial Filter

When asked to proof-read or polish content, you MUST apply these four filters sequentially:

### 1. The "Condescension" Check (Tone)
- **MUST NOT** use teacher-like framing (e.g., "You will learn to...", "What you need to understand is...", "It is important to note that...").
- **MUST** state the fascinating facts directly. Let the ideas do the work.
- **MUST** use an educational, active voice.

### 2. The "Cold Reader" Check (Jargon)
- **MUST** ensure the sentence makes sense to someone who has not read the source material or watched the video.
- **MUST NOT** drop unexplained jargon.
- *Example:* Change "hippocampal place cells fire" to "brain cells fire in a sequence matching the paths."

### 3. The "Visual" Check (Concrete Language)
- **MUST** replace abstract nouns with concrete, visual actions.
- **MUST** create an instant movie in the reader's head.
- *Example:* Change "vicarious trial and error" to "simulating the future before it moves."

### 4. The "Easy Wins" Check (Structure)
- **MUST** chunk text well. Use short paragraphs.
- **MUST** ensure hooks are actually hooking (e.g., placing the most surprising fact at the very beginning of the sentence).
- **MAY** use bulleted lists for rapid-fire surprises (e.g., "What you probably do not know yet").
- **MAY** use concrete analogies to explain abstract concepts (e.g., comparing cognitive offloading to GPS atrophy).

## Before / After Examples

**Before (Condescending & Jargon-heavy):**
> "You will learn that when a rat is at a maze junction, vicarious trial and error occurs as hippocampal place cells fire, which is a metaphor for planning."

**After (Direct & Visual):**
> "When a rat pauses at a maze junction, its brain cells fire in a sequence matching the paths it could take, simulating the future before it moves."

## Execution

When you run this skill:
1. Briefly state which of the 4 filters the original text failed.
2. Provide the rewritten text.
3. Ask the user if the new tone hits the mark.
